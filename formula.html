Northwestern Formula Racing


Formula SAE is an intercollegiate competition where students design, build, and compete using an F1 style open wheel racecar. Northwestern Formula Racing brings this competetive environment to the students at Northwestern University. Over my 3 years on the team I have worked primarily with the powertrain and electronics subteams, where we are responsible for making the car go fast without blowing up or tearing itself apart. Electronics


The only thing separating our cars from go-karts are their sophisticated electronics and controls (but don't tell the other subteams that). The wiring harness, sensors, communication network, and control logic represent a large portion of my contribution to the team. The process involves designing and updating the wiring harness to include important sensors, control systems, and actuators, continues with building and testing of individual modules, and concludes with assembly of the complete system. These modules include electro-pnuematic shifting, a multifunction steering wheel, power distribution, data acquisition, and an engine sensor/ECU harness.

Powertrain


The powertrain subteam is responsible for everything that makes the four stages of a four stroke engine work along with how the engine itself interacts with the rest of the car (drivetrain). Some of the projects I have worked on for powertrain include the intake design, gearing calculations, and especially calibration. My biggest role has been in the calibration area which involves a lengthy and iterative process of configuring the ECU with respect to ignition timing, fuel injector duty cycle, air pressure, and various other parameters from the variety of sensors available. With the right parameter settings, the engine can better utilize the air and fuel it combusts to make more power. The picture to the right shows the calibration process in action at a chassis dyno.
